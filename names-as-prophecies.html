The Genesis Genealogies · Abraham Park · Chapter 10

Ten Names,
One Story

From Adam to Noah — each name a witness. Each meaning, a glimpse of the One to come.

"The format of the genealogies and the meanings of the recorded names contain the hope and anticipation of how the redemptive promise would unfold with each successive generation and eventually be fulfilled through Jesus Christ." Abraham Park, The Genesis Genealogies

I
Adam
man · mankind · human
▾
The Name
The name Adam comes from the Assyrian root adamu, meaning "to make" or "to create." Man can never be the creator; he is only the creation and the creature. Adam was formed from the dust. Without the breath of God, the essence of man is only dust.
His Witness
Adam received God's promise — that the seed of the woman would bruise the head of the serpent. He cherished the garments of skin as his confirmation of the covenant, and testified to his descendants about the world before and after sin. He firmly believed the promised seed would come.
Points toward Christ
The sacrifice required to make garments of skin for Adam foreshadowed Jesus Christ's sacrifice on the cross. Adam's faith in the promised seed — "the seed of the woman" — was the first human witness to the coming Messiah. (Gen 3:15, 21)
"Adam cherished the garments of skin because they were his confirmation of the covenant. He firmly believed that the day would come when the promised seed would bruise the head of the serpent."Abraham Park, p. 95
II
Seth
substitute · granted · appointed one · foundation
▾
The Name
The name Seth is derived from a word meaning "to put" and "to set." Adam recognized that God had granted him another seed as part of His redemptive purpose. Seth was truly an expression of God's abounding grace — a gift of overflowing hope and comfort.
His Witness
Seth bore the image and likeness of Adam — demonstrating that God's image continued to pass through the line of faith, not the line of Cain. Through Seth, God's work was reestablished and deeply rooted. The promise would continue through his line.
Points toward Christ
Seth was "another seed" granted by God to replace Abel — a picture of Christ, the second Adam, who came to do what the first Adam could not. Just as Seth carried forward the image of God, Christ restores that image in all who believe. (Gen 4:25; 5:3; 1 Cor 15:45–47)
"Seth was truly an expression of God's abounding grace and a gift of overflowing hope and comfort."Abraham Park, p. 99
III
Enosh
man · mortal frailty · incurable
▾
The Name
The name Enosh is derived from a word meaning "incurable" and "very sick." It communicates man's total helplessness and predetermined frailty — like one groaning from an incurable illness. Seth named his son Enosh, recognizing man's frailty without God.
His Witness
During Enosh's time, men began to call upon the name of the Lord. Out of acknowledged weakness came true worship. The name Enosh was a confession of faith — and from that confession, the practice of calling upon God spread even to far regions.
Points toward Christ
The act of calling upon the name of the Lord, born from Enosh's generation, points directly to salvation through Christ — "Whoever will call upon the name of the Lord will be saved." Man's incurable condition requires a healer only God can send. (Gen 4:26; Acts 2:21; Rom 10:13)
"The name communicates man's total helplessness and predetermined frailty. It likens the state of sinful man to the condition of one groaning in sickness and affliction from an incurable illness."Abraham Park, p. 102
IV
Kenan
a child begotten · possession · nest
▾
The Name
The name Kenan shares the root with the word "nest" — to make a nest. His time was when the people finally built a nest of faith. Kenan probably was an unexpected gift from God, a son of great joy born through His providence.
His Witness
Park states: "Kenan means 'mercy unexpectedly found.'" The movement of worship begun by Enosh's generation found its settled home — a community of faith rooted in the mercy of God. What was scattered became gathered.
Points toward Christ
The unexpected mercy found in Kenan points to Jesus Christ — God's unexpected gift to mankind. "God had mercy on us and gave a great gift called Jesus Christ." It is unexpected because salvation is granted without merit, entirely by grace. (Gen 5:9–14)
"Kenan probably was an unexpected gift from God, a son of great joy born through His providence. God had mercy on us and gave a great gift called Jesus Christ."Abraham Park, p. 105
V
Mahalalel
praise of God · God be praised · glory to God
▾
The Name
Mahalalel is a compound word: mahalal (praise) + El (God). It means "praise of God" or "God be praised." The act of formal worship begun by Enosh evolved further into congregational praise — worship filled with glory in which God may dwell.
His Witness
Only God, who governs all history, is worthy to receive true praise. Praise is a force that unleashes God's power — it brought down the walls of Jericho, drove out evil spirits, opened prison gates. Mahalalel's name confesses that God alone deserves all glory.
Points toward Christ
The praise woven into this name finds its ultimate object in Christ. Revelation 5:12 — "Worthy is the Lamb that was slain to receive power and riches and wisdom and strength and honor and glory and blessing." All praise leads to Him. (Rev 5:12; 19:1–5)
"Only God, who governs all history, is worthy to receive true praise and worship."Abraham Park, p. 107
VI
Jared
descent · to carry down · to come down from a higher place
▾
The Name
The name Jared originates from the Hebrew word meaning "to carry down," "to transport," or "to put down." The spiritual movement to praise God had not been limited to a few, but was widespread — the practice of worship had been successfully passed down through the generations.
His Witness
The alternative meaning of Jared is "descendant" — pointing to the longing for the seed of the woman. The name carries a hope embedded in its very root: that God, worthy of all praise, would come down into this world and save mankind from below.
Points toward Christ
The meaning "to come down" anticipates the Incarnation — "The Word became flesh and dwelt among us." God came down. The hope embedded in Jared's name was fulfilled when Jesus Christ, the Word incarnate, came into this world. (John 1:14; Gen 5:15–20)
"The longing for the woman's seed is also evident from the alternative meaning of the name Jared: 'descendant.' As the fulfillment of this hope, Jesus Christ, the Word incarnate, came into this world and dwelt among the people."Abraham Park, p. 110
VII
Enoch
dedicated · offered · initiated · teacher
▾
The Name
The name Enoch is the noun form of a verb meaning "to offer." The most common rendering is "offered" and "dedicated one." Its root means "to inspire" or "to teach." Jared named his son "dedicated" out of the desire to fully dedicate him to God as the first fruit of his godly life.
His Witness
Enoch walked with God 300 years and did not see death — "God took him." In a genealogy marked eight times by the phrase "and he died," Enoch alone broke the pattern. He demonstrated the eternal life that would have been possible if Adam had not sinned.
Points toward Christ
Enoch was a foreshadow of Jesus Christ, the Lamb who was fully dedicated before God to die on the cross as an atoning sacrifice. And his defeat of death points to Christ's resurrection — the one who forever overcame the power of death for all who believe. (Gen 5:21–24; John 1:29; 1 Cor 5:7)
"Enoch was a foreshadow of Jesus Christ, the Lamb who was fully dedicated before God to die on the cross as an atoning sacrifice for the sins of mankind."Abraham Park, p. 113
VIII
Methuselah
when he dies, judgment shall come
▾
The Name
Methuselah's name means "when he is dead, it shall come." Enoch received this as divine revelation at his son's birth — the flood would come after Methuselah's death. He lived 969 years, the longest of any human, and died in the very year the flood came.
His Witness
Enoch named his son according to the Word to prepare faith that is ready for the end. The awareness of coming judgment — carried in the very name Methuselah called upon daily — prompted Enoch to walk with God. Methuselah's birth was God's revelation and sign of the judgment by flood.
Points toward Christ
The judgment written into Methuselah's name ultimately pointed to the final judgment that Christ would bear on the cross — and to the coming day when He will judge all. His longevity speaks of God's patience: every year was grace extended. God is not slow, but patient toward all. (2 Pet 3:9; Gen 5:25–27; 7:11)
"Methuselah's birth was God's revelation and sign of the judgment by flood."Abraham Park, p. 120
IX
Lamech
powerful one · strong youth · a man of prayer
▾
The Name
Lamech of Seth's line was a godly man — a man of prayer. He did not rebel against God under the duress of suffering and affliction; rather, these things motivated his faith. He trusted solely in God and hoped that the curse would be lifted through his son.
His Witness
Lamech earnestly anticipated the coming of the Messiah. He longed for the end of man's sorrowful fallen state and the restoration of Eden. He hoped that someone would lift the death sentence declared upon man and open the path back to God — and named his son accordingly.
Points toward Christ
Lamech's confession — "This one shall give us rest from our work and from the toil of our hands" — is a direct Messianic prophecy. Park notes he may have thought his son Noah was the Messiah. He was mistaken in the person, but not in the longing — which was finally fulfilled in Christ. (Gen 5:29; Isa 9:6; John 14:27)
"This Lamech was a godly man. He did not rebel against God or give up under the duress of suffering and affliction; rather, these things motivated his faith. He trusted solely in God and hoped that the curse would be lifted through his son."Abraham Park, p. 123
X
Noah
rest · comfort · consolation
▾
The Name
The name Noah derives from a Hebrew word meaning "to settle down," "appease," or "rest." The derived meanings are "Sabbath," "rest," and "comfort." The godly descendants longed earnestly for God's comfort through the birth of this son.
His Witness
The name is "a reflection of a newly revealed providence of redemption from God." The end to the long period of suffering had come; it was now time for God's comfort and rest. Noah was a type — a foreshadow — of the Savior who gives true comfort to this earth.
Points toward Christ
Noah was a type of Jesus Christ, whom God had sent. As Noah bore his family through judgment into a new world, Christ bears all who trust Him through judgment into eternal life. "Jesus Christ is the one who gives true comfort and rest to this earth." (Gen 5:29; Isa 9:6; John 14:27)
"The name is a reflection of a newly revealed providence of redemption from God. The end to the long period of suffering had come; it was now time for God's comfort and rest."Abraham Park, p. 127
What the Genealogy Carries
Ten generations.
One unbroken witness.
These are not merely the records of men who lived and died. Park writes that the genealogies were composed so that the meanings of the recorded names would carry "the hope and anticipation of how the redemptive promise would unfold with each successive generation."
Each name is a word spoken across centuries — from a father's faith, into his son's identity, toward a coming Savior none of them fully saw. Together they form an unbroken chain of witnesses from Adam to the flood, testifying that God never lost hold of the thread.
